Biography

American violinist Amy Sze made her Carnegie debut at the age of 16 shortly after performing the Barber Violin Concerto with Edwin Outwater and the Eastern Sierra Symphony. She began playing the violin at the age of 3 and recently graduated from the Juilliard School where she studied with Professor Li Lin as a recipient of the Lily Z. Foldes Award. She is now studying with Martin Beaver as a graduate student of the Colburn Conservatory.

Amy has been a featured soloist with numerous orchestras including the Santa Maria Philharmonic, Eastern Sierra Symphony, Palisades Symphony, Antelope Valley Symphony, Mount Saint Mary’s Orchestra, among others. She was most recently selected to compete in the Isang Yun International Violin Competition and had won first prize in the Young Muse International Strings Competition, the Classics Alive National Auditions, and was a semifinalist in both the Stradivarius International Violin Competition and M-Prize International Chamber Music Competition.

Amy studied and performed on full scholarship at the Moritzburg Festival in Dresden, Germany where she was the youngest member, associate concertmaster, and winner of the audience choice award. She also attended the Morningside Music Bridge Festival in Warsaw, Poland on full scholarship where she studied with Midori, Noah Bendix-Balgley, and Ning Feng, as well as the National Arts Centre’s Young Artists Program in Ottawa, Canada, where she was a featured performer on CBC Radio One.

She has also worked closely with prominent artists such as Rohan De Silva, Glenn Dicterow, Joseph Kalichstein, Martin Chalifour, and Alexander Treger. As a passionate chamber musician, she was selected for the Shenandoah String Quartet Program at the Heifetz International Music Institute, working with members of the Emerson, Borromeo, and American String Quartets. As a fellow at the Music Academy of the West, she performed the world premiere of Tom Cipullo’s “BonFire Opera” alongside mezzo-soprano Sasha Cooke and pianist John Churchwell.

She was invited to participate in the 2019 Starling-Delay Symposium at the Juilliard School. Amy is also an avid jazz violinist and has performed with French violinist Gilles Apap and jazz pianist Matthew Whitaker. Amy’s violin and bow are generously on loan from the Maestro Foundation.
